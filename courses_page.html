cybersecurity Activities

#1. Scanning Activities

Scanning activities, in the context of cybersecurity, refer to the systematic process of examining computer networks, systems, or applications to identify vulnerabilities, weaknesses, or potential security threats. These activities involve the use of specialized tools and techniques to probe and analyze various components of an organization's digital infrastructure.

#2. Honeypot

A honeypot is a cybersecurity technique that involves creating a decoy system, network, or application to attract and deceive malicious actors. It is designed to simulate a vulnerable or enticing target that appears attractive to attackers, diverting their attention and gathering information about their tactics, techniques, and motives.

#3. Distributed Denial of Service Attack

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack is a malicious act in which multiple compromised systems, often forming a botnet, are used to flood a target system, network, or website with an overwhelming amount of traffic or requests. The intention behind a DDoS attack is to exhaust the target's resources, such as bandwidth, processing power, or memory, rendering it inaccessible to legitimate users.

#4. Digitalization

Digitalization refers to the process of transforming analog or physical information, systems, processes, and interactions into digital form, making them accessible, manageable, and usable through digital technologies. It involves leveraging digital tools, technologies, and platforms to streamline operations, improve efficiency, enhance customer experiences, and enable new business models.

#5. OWASP and IEEE Top 10

OWASP (Open Web Application Security Project) and IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) are two prominent organizations in the field of cybersecurity, each contributing to different aspects of the industry.

#6. Solar Winds Attack

The SolarWinds attack, also known as the SolarWinds supply chain attack or the Sunburst attack, refers to a highly sophisticated cyberattack that occurred in late 2020. The attack involved compromising the software supply chain of SolarWinds, a leading provider of IT management and monitoring solutions, specifically targeting their Orion platform.
